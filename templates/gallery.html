To finish growing your tobacco crop, ensure it receives consistent watering (about 1-1.5 inches per week) while avoiding overwatering to prevent disease. Monitor for pests and diseases like aphids and blue mold, applying treatments as needed. Around this stage, topping and removing suckers will help direct energy into leaf growth instead of flowers. As harvest approaches, watch for leaves turning lighter green or slightly yellow, which signals they are ready for curing.
